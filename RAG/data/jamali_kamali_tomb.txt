Location: Mehrauli, Delhi, India

History:
The Jamali Kamali complex was built in the 16th century during the early Mughal period. It contains a mosque and tomb dedicated to the Sufi saint Jamali and his companion Kamali.

Architecture:
Features Mughal architecture with red sandstone, marble decoration, domes, and beautiful inscriptions.

Cultural Significance:
Important site reflecting Sufi culture and Islamic spiritual traditions.

Tourism Information:
Located within Mehrauli Archaeological Park.

Interesting Facts:
The monument is also known for its artistic interior ornamentation.
