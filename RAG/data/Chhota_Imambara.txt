Location: Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India

History:
Built in 1838 by Nawab Muhammad Ali Shah as a congregation hall for Shia Muslims.

Architecture:
Indo-Islamic architecture with golden domes, chandeliers, and decorative interiors.

Cultural Significance:
Used for religious ceremonies, especially during Muharram.

Tourism Information:
Popular historical monument in Lucknow.

Interesting Facts:
Often called the “Palace of Lights” due to its illuminated decorations during festivals.
