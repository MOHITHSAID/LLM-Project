Location: Delhi, India

History:
Alai Minar was commissioned by Alauddin Khilji in the early 14th century. It was intended to be twice the height of Qutub Minar, symbolizing the ruler’s ambition and power.

Architecture:
The tower remains incomplete, with only the first massive circular storey constructed. It is made mainly of rough stone masonry.

Cultural Significance:
It reflects the grand architectural ambitions of the Delhi Sultanate period.

Tourism Information:
Located within the Qutub complex and visited along with Qutub Minar.

Interesting Facts:
Construction stopped after Alauddin Khilji’s death, leaving the structure unfinished.
