Location: Qutub Complex, Delhi, India

History:
The Iron Pillar dates back to around the 4th century CE during the Gupta Empire, most likely built in honor of King Chandragupta II.

Architecture:
Made of nearly pure iron, about 7 meters tall, with Sanskrit inscriptions carved on its surface.

Cultural Significance:
Famous for its remarkable resistance to corrosion despite being exposed to the environment for centuries.

Tourism Information:
Located near Qutub Minar and frequently visited by tourists and researchers.

Interesting Facts:
It is studied worldwide for ancient Indian metallurgical expertise.
