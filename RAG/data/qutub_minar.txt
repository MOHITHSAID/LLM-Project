Location: Delhi, India

History:
Construction started in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak after the Delhi Sultanate was established. Later rulers completed additional floors.

Architecture:
It stands about 73 meters tall and is made of red sandstone and marble. The tower has carved inscriptions from the Quran and decorative balconies.

Cultural Significance:
It is part of a UNESCO heritage complex and represents early Islamic architecture in India.

Tourism Information:
Popular tourist site in Delhi with nearby monuments like Iron Pillar and Alai Darwaza.

Interesting Facts:
It is one of the tallest brick minarets in the world.
