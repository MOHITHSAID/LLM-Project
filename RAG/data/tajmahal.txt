Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

History:
The Taj Mahal was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Construction began in 1632 and took about 20â€“22 years to complete. Thousands of artisans and craftsmen worked on this monument.

Architecture:
It is made primarily of white marble with intricate carvings, precious stone inlay work, and symmetrical gardens. The central dome is surrounded by four minarets. The design reflects Mughal, Persian, and Indian architectural influences.

Cultural Significance:
It is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It symbolizes love and architectural excellence.

Tourism Information:
The best time to visit is October to March. It attracts millions of visitors every year.

Interesting Facts:
The monument appears to change color depending on sunlight and time of day.
