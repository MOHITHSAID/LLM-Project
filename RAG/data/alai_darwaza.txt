Location: Delhi, India

History:
Alai Darwaza was built in 1311 by Sultan Alauddin Khilji as part of the expansion of the Qutub Minar complex. It served as the southern gateway to the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque.

Architecture:
The monument is constructed using red sandstone and white marble. It displays Indo-Islamic architecture with intricate geometric patterns, Islamic calligraphy, and decorative arches.

Cultural Significance:
It represents the early development of Islamic architecture in India and showcases advanced construction techniques of the Delhi Sultanate period.

Tourism Information:
It is part of the Qutub Minar heritage complex, which attracts many tourists every year.

Interesting Facts:
It is considered one of the earliest true domes built in India using Islamic architectural principles.
